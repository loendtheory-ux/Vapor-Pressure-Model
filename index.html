EVAPORATION

High-energy liquid molecules at the surface overcome intermolecular forces and escape into the gas phase. This happens continuously — even below boiling point.

🔵

CONDENSATION

Gas molecules slow down, lose energy to collisions, and are recaptured by intermolecular forces — returning to the liquid. Rate depends on vapor molecule concentration.

⇌

DYNAMIC EQUILIBRIUM

In a closed container, evaporation and condensation rates become equal. Vapor molecule count stays constant. The pressure of that vapor = vapor pressure.

📊

WHAT AFFECTS VP?

Temperature ↑ → VP ↑: More KE, more escapes. Weaker IMFs → Higher VP (more volatile). VP does not depend on amount of liquid or surface area.

★ Regents Key Takeaways

  • Vapor pressure is measured in a closed container at equilibrium
  • Temperature ↑ → VP ↑ (always, for all substances)
  • Substances with weak IMFs (nonpolar, small) have high VP (volatile)
  • At the boiling point, VP = external (atmospheric) pressure
  • VP does NOT depend on the quantity of liquid or gas present
  • Use the Reference Tables (Table H) for VP vs. temperature graphs
  • Ethanol > water in VP — weaker hydrogen bonding than water
  • VP is a property of the substance + temperature only
