EVAPORATION

High-energy surface molecules overcome IMFs and escape to gas phase. Rate depends on temperature and IMF strength — weaker IMFs = higher evap rate.

🔵

CONDENSATION

Vapor molecules lose KE and are recaptured by IMFs. Rate depends on vapor concentration — more vapor = more surface collisions.

⇌

DYNAMIC EQUILIBRIUM

In a closed container, rates become equal. Constant vapor pressure = vapor pressure. Higher temp or weaker IMFs → higher VP.

📊

TABLE H ORDER

Propanone > Ethanol > Water > Ethanoic acid in VP at any temperature. Propanone has weakest IMFs; ethanoic acid has strongest.

Regents Key Takeaways

  • VP measured in a closed container at equilibrium
  • Temp up = VP up for all liquids
  • Weak IMFs = High VP (more volatile)
  • At boiling point: VP = atmospheric pressure
  • VP does NOT depend on quantity of liquid
  • Use Table H: propanone, ethanol, water, ethanoic acid
  • Propanone boils ~56C, ethanol ~78C, water 100C, ethanoic acid ~118C
  • VP is a property of substance + temperature only
